About Adidas

Adidas, founded by Adi Dassler in 1949, has evolved from a small shoe factory in Germany to one of the world's leading sportswear brands, known for its innovation and iconic three-stripe logo.

Founding and Early Years

Adidas traces its roots back to 1924 when Adi Dassler and his brother Rudolf established the Gebrüder Dassler Schuhfabrik (Dassler Brothers Shoe Factory) in Herzogenaurach, Germany. The company initially focused on producing high-quality athletic shoes. Adi's innovative designs gained recognition, particularly when Jesse Owens wore Dassler shoes during the 1936 Berlin Olympics, winning four gold medals and bringing international attention to the brand.

The Birth of Adidas

In 1949, following a personal rift between the brothers, Adi Dassler registered the Adolf Dassler adidas Sportschuhfabrik, officially marking the birth of Adidas. The name "Adidas" is derived from the first three letters of Adi's first name and the first three letters of his last name. The iconic three-stripe logo was acquired from the Finnish sports company Karhu Sports in 1952, which became synonymous with the brand.
